Scarlet Letter Analysis

Introduction:

Throughout the year, I have written many essays, but this essay from second quarter stands apart from the rest. For the assignment, I wrote about Roger Chillingworth and how he was developed as a dark character through diction. This essay was significant for me because it was the first time I had to analyze a book so thoroughly.

Scarlet Letter Analysis

Reflection

In this assignment, I wrote about how Nathaniel Hawthorne described the change in character of Roger Chillingworth in The Scarlet Letter. We were given several options to show the change, such as diction, tone, and other literary devices. I chose to examine diction used within the novel and noticed significant changes in the way Chillingworth was described and the settings he was placed in. The main issue I came across in this asignment was finding information. I knew what I was looking for, but had no previous experience with analyzing a book. Reading through it, I realize there are many things that could be improved upon, and have improved in my later writings.

Introduction:

One of the most informative assignments of the year was the Columnist Project. In this assignment, I chose the analyst Gail Collins, who writes for The New York Times. By completing this asignment, I learned that there are bits of bias in many news stories.

Columnist Project

Reflection

Before I started this project, I had no idea what it would end up becoming. In doing this assignment, I learned a lot about the white house and it's current operations. In addition, I learned about bias in the media and how sides can be presented differently through different appeals. Introduction:

This project was notable because it was the first time I had to do a research project like this. In this assignment, I researched the topic of acid rain, and learned about the many effects it has on the environment. I had to choose 4 sources total, two opinion pieces and two scientific articles.

Mini Research Project

Reflection

Before starting this project, I knew nothing about the effects of acid rain. Through this assignment, I learned a lot about the environment and the dangers it faces, as well as how to research for a project. This assignment was a fun mix of science and writing to produce a well-researched essay.

Looking back at the project, I realize that I could have done better choosing and incorporating sources. Many of my sources were weak and hard to properly summzrize within the project. I believe I did well for my first time and believe I would be able to do even better next time.

DCQ Essay

Introduction:

By far, the assignment I'm most proud of is the DCQ Essay. In this essay, I had to pick a quote and choose whether to defend it, challenge it, or quantify it. For this essay, I picked the Henry David Thoreau quote "The only obligation which I have a right to assume is to do at any time what I think is right." and argued why I disagreed with it.

DCQ Essay

Reflection

This essay was the last of the school year, and stands out as my best work, and the writing I'm most proud of. In writing this essay, I spent a lot of time going over peer reviews and making changes to better fit the thesis.

Comparing this essay to my previous works, I can see I've greatly improved my writing and comprehension. This essay has very few mistakes, while the others were littered with them, and I'm proud of the progress it shows. While I have not gotten a grade for this essay yet, I hope that the amount of effort I put in is reflected in the grade.
